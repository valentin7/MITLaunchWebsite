High School Entrepreneurship Program

We create ENTREPRENEURS.

Start a company in a month

MIT Launch is a 4-week entrepreneurship summer program for high school students.

Our entrepreneurs will start a company in teams, pitching to a panel of judges and investors at the end of the summer. Classes are taught by MIT and Harvard Business School students and alumni, successful entrepreneurs, and professors from top tier schools. Teams are also given a network of mentors with a wealth of business and entrepreneurship experience to help guide and support them during the startup process.

Location

The program is held at Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Cambridge, Massachusetts. Students live in the MIT dorms and classes are held in MIT classrooms.

Tuition

Estimated cost for the program is $5,985, including tuition, housing, and activities for the duration of the program. Many need based scholarships are available.

Launch Blog

Discover more about the summer program experience through our blog that chronicles some of the class experiences,students' lives, businesses, and other adventures along the way.

About Us

Launch enables high school students to launch a company, by providing the resources needed to go from "idea" to "implementation." For each of our students, we instill a self-starter mentality, teach tangible, practical business skills, and introduce entrepreneurship as a career path. We aspire for our programming to create students with more adaptability and resourcefulness geared towards the real world, develop and inspire more makers, and generate more demand for entrepreneurship education in our schools.
